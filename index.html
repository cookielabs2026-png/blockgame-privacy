Privacy Policy for BlockGame

Last updated: June 29, 2026

BlockGame ("the app", "we", "us") is a single-player, offline game published by Jeremy Stinchecum. This policy explains how the app handles information. The short version: BlockGame does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data whatsoever.

Information we collect

None. BlockGame does not collect any personal or sensitive user data. We do not ask for your name, email, location, contacts, photos, or any account information. The app does not include any analytics, advertising, or tracking software.

Data stored on your device

The app saves your game progress (your worlds, inventory, and settings) locally on your own device using the browser-style local storage built into the app. This data never leaves your device, is never uploaded anywhere, and is not accessible to us or to any third party. Uninstalling the app or clearing its data removes it permanently.

Internet and permissions

BlockGame is designed to run fully offline and does not require an internet connection to play. The app does not transmit any information over the internet.

Children's privacy

BlockGame is suitable for all ages, including children. Because the app collects no data of any kind, it does not knowingly collect information from children or anyone else. The app complies with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and Google Play's Families policy by collecting no data.

Third-party services

The app does not integrate any third-party services, SDKs, advertising networks, or analytics providers.

Changes to this policy

If the app changes in a way that affects this policy (for example, if optional ads or purchases are added in a future version), this policy will be updated and the new version will be posted at this same address before the change takes effect.
